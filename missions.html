Pakistan's Space Missions

Journey through SUPARCO's remarkable achievements

Space Mission Timeline

1961

SUPARCO Established

Space & Upper Atmosphere Research Commission founded, making Pakistan one of the first Asian countries with a dedicated space agency.

1962

First Rocket Launch - Rehbar-I

Pakistan successfully launched its first rocket, marking the beginning of practical space exploration activities in South Asia.

1990

Badr-1 Satellite

Pakistan's first satellite launched into orbit from China. It was an experimental satellite weighing 52 kg for digital communications.

2001

Badr-B Satellite

Second satellite launched for scientific research and development, enhancing Pakistan's space capabilities.

2011

PakSat-1R Communication Satellite

Advanced communication satellite providing TV broadcasting, internet, and telecommunication services across Pakistan.

2018

PRSS-1 & PakTES-1A

Remote Sensing Satellite System for earth observation, agriculture monitoring, and disaster management launched successfully.

2026

PakSat-MM1 (Upcoming)

Next-generation multi-mission satellite planned for launch, expanding communication and observation capabilities significantly.
